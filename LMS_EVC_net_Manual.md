# Last Mile Solutions EVC-net Manual

## Introduction

Welcome to the comprehensive manual for Last Mile Solutions' EVC-net platform. This guide is designed for both Administrators and Customers to help you navigate, configure, and manage your electric vehicle charging infrastructure effectively.

## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 First Time Log In As System User

To get started with EVC-net, you will need a System User account.
**Requirements:**
- System User account in EVC-net
- Access to a Computer and Mobile Phone
- An Authenticator App (e.g., Microsoft Authenticator, Google Authenticator)

**Steps to Log In:**
1.  **Set Up Password:** You will receive an email with a link. Click it to create your password and click "Reset".
2.  **Sign On:** Go to the login page, enter your email/username and the new password.
3.  **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):**
    - On your first login, click "No authentication code".
    - You will receive a one-time identification code via email. Enter this code to proceed.
    - Once logged in, you will be redirected to your admin profile (or click your name -> Your user data) to see a QR code for 2FA setup.
    - Scan the QR code with your Authenticator App.
    - For future logins, use the code generated by your app.

### 1.2 Creating a System User

Super System Users can create other System User accounts.
1.  Navigate to **Settings** > **System Users**.
2.  Click **New**.
3.  Fill in the required details (Name, Email, Role, etc.).
4.  Save the new user. They will receive an activation email.

### 1.3 Roles Overview

EVC-net uses roles to define permissions:
-   **Super System Administration:** Full access to everything (Dashboard, Charge Points, Cards, Customers, Payments, Settings, etc.).
-   **System Administration:** Full access similar to Super Admin but may have some restrictions on specific system-wide settings.
-   **Card Management:** Access to Tickets, Cards, and Customers.
-   **Technical Service:** Access to Charge Points, Settings, SIM cards, and Tickets.
-   **Invoice Only:** Access to invoices.
-   **Read-only:** View-only access to most modules.
-   **Management:** Access to Dashboard, Reports, and Tickets.
-   **Control Room:** Access and editing rights for Charge Points and Tickets.

## 2. Charge Points Management

### 2.1 Charge Point Elements (Attachments)

Before activating a charge point, four key elements ("attachments") must be configured:
1.  **Charge Group:** Defines access policies (who can charge) and tariffs. Mandatory for every charge point.
2.  **Product (Subscription):** Defines the hosting subscription based on location type (Private, Business, Public), parent type (Master/Slave), and socket count.
3.  **Location:** Specifies the physical address and publishing status (visible in apps/maps).
4.  **Customer:** Associated account roles:
    -   *Owner:* View access.
    -   *Payer Subscription:* Pays hosting fees.
    -   *Receiver Charge Transactions:* Receives reimbursement for energy costs (e.g., for home chargers).

### 2.2 Setting Up a New Charge Point

1.  **Register:** Ensure the charge point is registered in the system (Status: "Registered CP").
2.  **Configure Attachments:** Assign the Charge Group, Product, Location, and Customer as described above.
3.  **Activate:** Once configured, change the status to "Activated CP".
4.  **Connect:** The charge point should now come online ("Connected CP").

### 2.3 Managing Charge Points

-   **Status Tab:** Activate/Deactivate charge points. Deactivation terminates subscriptions.
-   **Subscriptions Tab:** View current, past, and future subscriptions. Changes take effect the first day of the next month.
-   **Access Tab:** Manage the attached Charge Group.
-   **Real-time Dashboard:** Monitor channel status (Green=Available, Blue=Charging, Red=Error, Gray=Offline).

### 2.4 Remote Control

From the "Real-time dashboard" tab, you can perform remote actions:
-   **Hard/Soft Reset:** Restart the station.
-   **Unlock Connector:** Release a stuck cable.
-   **Start/Stop Transaction:** Manually control charging sessions.
-   **Get Diagnostics:** Retrieve logs for troubleshooting.
-   **Block/Unblock:** Disable or enable the use of the charge point.

## 3. Charge Cards Management

### 3.1 Types of Charge Cards

-   **RFID Cards/Key Fobs:** Physical tokens used to authenticate at the charger.
-   **Virtual Tokens:** App-based tokens for authentication without physical cards.
-   **Admin/Service Cards:** Maintenance cards used for testing and service, accepted on all chargers within the network.

### 3.2 Adding a New Charge Card

1.  Navigate to **Charge Cards**.
2.  Click **New**.
3.  Enter the **Contract ID** (UID printed on the card).
4.  Assign it to a **Customer** (or create a new one).
5.  Set the status to **Active**.

### 3.3 Ordering & Importing

-   **Ordering:** You can order new cards/fobs directly through Last Mile Solutions or supported 3rd parties.
-   **Bulk Import:** For large numbers of cards, use the "Request Import" feature or contact support to process a bulk upload file.

## 4. Customer Management

### 4.1 Customer Types

-   **Private User:** Individual EV drivers (e.g., residential).
-   **Company:** Business entities managing fleets or locations.
-   **Employee:** Drivers linked to a Company account (often for home reimbursement or fleet cards).

### 4.2 Creating Accounts

**Private User:**
1.  Navigate to **Customers**.
2.  Select **New Private User**.
3.  Fill in personal details and address.
4.  Save.

**Company Account:**
1.  Navigate to **Customers**.
2.  Select **New Company**.
3.  Enter company details (VAT, address, billing info).
4.  Create a primary Company Admin user.

**Employee Account:**
Typically created by the Company Admin, but System Users can also assist. Requires linking to the parent Company account.

## 5. Home Reimbursement

### 5.1 Concept

Home Reimbursement allows employees charging company cars at home to be reimbursed for the energy cost.
-   **Payer:** Employer (pays for the charging session).
-   **Receiver:** Employee (receives payment for electricity used).

### 5.2 Setup Guide

1.  **Charge Point Setup:**
    -   Location must be "Private".
    -   *Receiver Charge Transactions* role on the Charge Point must be the Employee.
    -   *Payer Subscription* is typically the Employer (Company).
2.  **Card Setup:**
    -   Employee uses a charge card linked to the Company (Employer pays).
3.  **Tariff:**
    -   A specific "Home Tariff" is applied to calculate the reimbursement amount.
    -   Transactions are automatically tracked and processed for split billing.

## 6. Products, Tariffs & Margins

### 6.1 Products

Products represent subscriptions for services like:
-   **Charge Point Hosting:** Monthly fee for connecting a charger (varies by Private/Public/Business).
-   **Charge Card Subscription:** Monthly fee for active charge cards.

To request new products, use the PTM (Products, Tariffs, Margins) form provided by your onboarding specialist.

### 6.2 Tariffs & Margins

-   **Tariffs:** The rate charged for electricity (per kWh, per minute, or per session).
-   **Margins (Surcharges):** Additional fees added on top of the base tariff by the Mobility Service Provider (MSP) or Charge Point Operator (CPO).

New tariffs or margin configurations must be requested via the standard PTM request forms.

## 7. Support & Tickets

### 7.1 Ticket System

-   **Automatic Tickets:** Charge points can automatically generate tickets when errors occur (enable "Automatic Tickets" in Charge Point Data).
-   **Manual Tickets:** Customers or System Users can create tickets for inquiries or issues.
-   **Management:** View and respond to tickets in the **Tickets** section.
